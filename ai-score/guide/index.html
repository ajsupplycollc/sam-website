What the AI Visibility Scorer Checks — and How to Fix Each

The big idea

People used to Google you and click your site. Now ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google's AI answer the question for them — and only recommend a handful of businesses. This tool checks whether AI can find you, understand you, trust you, and recommend you. Two parts: AI Search Readiness (can AI use your site) and Web Foundation (the basics AI and customers both expect).

Part 1 — AI Search Readiness (AEO / GEO)
The signals that decide whether AI pulls your business into its answers.
1. AI crawlers allowed highest weight
AI search engines send bots (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google's AI) to read your site. A file called robots.txt can accidentally block them — meaning you're invisible to AI no matter how good your site is.
Fix: Edit robots.txt to explicitly allow the AI bots. One small file change; instantly makes you readable.
2. Structured data (schema)
Hidden labels in your code that tell AI exactly what you are — a business, your services, hours, reviews — instead of making it guess from your text.
Fix: Add schema.org markup (JSON-LD) for your organization, services and reviews.
3. Business identity schema (LocalBusiness)
The specific label that says "this is a real business named X, in city Y, phone Z." This is what feeds local AI answers like "best plumber near me."
Fix: Add LocalBusiness schema with your name, address, phone and hours.
4. FAQ / question-answer content
AI answers ARE questions. Pages that clearly ask and answer real customer questions get pulled straight into AI results.
Fix: Add an FAQ section answering what customers actually ask, tagged with FAQPage schema.
5. Meta description (your answer snippet)
The one- or two-sentence summary of your page. AI and search use it as your preview line.
Fix: Write a clear 1–2 sentence description of what you do and who you serve.
6. Social / preview tags (OpenGraph)
Tags that control how your link looks when shared and give AI extra context about the page.
Fix: Add og:title, og:description and og:image tags.
7. llms.txt file
A brand-new standard file that hands AI a clean, plain summary of your site — an early "we're AI-ready" signal most sites don't have yet.
Fix: Add an llms.txt file at your domain root. (Ours has one — few competitors do.)
8. Sitemap
A map of all your pages so crawlers find everything, not just the homepage.
Fix: Generate sitemap.xml and link it in robots.txt.
9. Enough real content
Thin pages give AI nothing to quote. The more real, useful content, the more there is for AI to pull and cite.
Fix: Add substantive content — services explained, FAQs, an about section.
10. One clear headline (H1)
A single main headline tells AI the page's topic. None, or several, confuses it.
Fix: Use exactly one H1 per page that states what the page is about.
11. Stats & data research-backed
A Princeton study found pages with concrete numbers get cited by AI 30–40% more. Data reads as credible.
Fix: Add real numbers — years in business, jobs completed, response times, results.
12. Quotes & sources research-backed
The same study found expert quotes and citations can boost AI citation up to +115%. It signals authority.
Fix: Add customer testimonials, expert quotes, or references to trusted sources.
13. Visible author & dates (trust signals)
AI weighs who's behind the content and how fresh it is (part of what Google calls E-E-A-T).
Fix: Show your business/author name and published or updated dates.
Part 2 — Web Foundation
The basics AI and human customers both expect. Break these and nothing above matters.
14. HTTPS / SSL (the padlock)
Secure connection. Sites without it get distrusted and downranked by everyone.
Fix: Install an SSL certificate — usually free from your host.
15. Mobile-friendly
Whether the site is built to work on phones, where most customers actually are.
Fix: Add the mobile viewport tag and use responsive design.
16. Page title
The title shown in the browser tab and search results — a top-level signal of what you do.
Fix: Set a clear, descriptive title with your business + service + city.
17. Contact method
Can a customer (or AI) actually find how to reach you — a form, email or phone.
Fix: Add a visible contact form and/or email and phone number.
18. Social profiles linked
Linked, active social accounts confirm you're a real, current business.
Fix: Link your Instagram, Facebook, etc. from the site.
19. Image alt text
Text descriptions of your images so AI and screen readers understand them.
Fix: Add descriptive alt text to every meaningful image.
20. Canonical URL
Tells search which version of a page is the official one, preventing duplicate-content confusion.
Fix: Add a canonical link tag to each page.
